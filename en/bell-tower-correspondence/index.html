The Four Letters, in Full

Letter 1 Observer → Bell June 19, 2026

On June 19, the Observer filed a detailed written submission with Bell's agent, Robert Minotti (Romin Acquisition), before the close of the initial comment period. It organized its questions into seven areas:

  • Site selection and the alternatives Bell considered
  • Environmental and flood-zone status of the proposed site
  • The physical footprint of the tower and fenced compound
  • The consultation process and its timeline
  • Future expansion and co-location by other carriers
  • The proposed municipal lease
  • The supporting studies and records Bell relied on
Editorial note   The June 19 submission built on an earlier June 7 request for information the Observer had sent to both Bell and the Municipality, seeking clarification on alternative sites, the water-tower assessment, zoning compatibility, flood-zone mapping, the lease, and consultation procedure.
Letter 2 Bell → Observer June 19, 2026

Bell responded the same day. It confirmed several points and, importantly, set the reply deadline.

On the water tower — the only existing structure Bell's notification package identified as evaluated — Bell wrote that it "was evaluated by an independent engineering firm retained by Bell and was determined to be structurally unsuitable," adding that Bell "does not publicly disclose proprietary engineering analyses."

On the Municipality's role, Bell confirmed:

"The Municipality of Ormstown participated in discussions regarding potential locations and collaborated in the selection of the preferred site." — Bell (Robert Minotti), June 19, 2026

Bell also confirmed the correct cadastral number for the site is Lot 6 065 483 — resolving a discrepancy with the number stated at the March 2 council meeting — and directed cadastral and zoning questions to the Municipality. Finally, Bell identified July 10, 2026 as the deadline for reply comments within the 21-day reply period.

Editorial note   This is the letter in which Bell itself establishes the July 10 reply deadline — the date that becomes central to Letter 4.
Letter 3 Observer → Bell June 25, 2026

Six days later, the Observer filed a follow-up, expressly within the reply period Bell had opened, raising eight specific points that Bell's first response had not fully answered. The letter opened:

"I am submitting this follow-up within the 21-day reply period referenced in Bell's consultation package and in CPC-2-0-03." — Observer to Bell, June 25, 2026

Among the eight points, the Observer asked Bell to reconcile its two positions on the water-tower engineering report (available at the June 10 session, or proprietary?); clarified that its question about privately owned candidate sites sought only non-identifying, aggregate information — general location, site type, approximate distance, or technical reason for rejection — not the names of any landowners; and asked whether any of the other three or four municipal lots were zoned for a use less sensitive than park and green space, and whether those alternatives were technically feasible.

On the timing, the Observer asked directly:

"Please clarify Bell's position on whether written concurrence from the Municipality before July 10 would be procedurally premature under CPC-2-0-03." — Observer to Bell, June 25, 2026
Letter 4 Bell → Observer June 26, 2026

Bell responded the next day, June 26, addressing the eight points — and then closing the record.

On alternative sites and Annex A, Bell set out its interpretation of what it was required to disclose:

"Additional candidate locations considered during the site selection process consisted of potential greenfield locations rather than existing antenna systems or structures contemplated by Annex A. The identification, evaluation and negotiation of potential candidate sites form part of Bell's confidential commercial and engineering site selection process and are not disclosure requirements under CPC-2-0-03." — Bell (Robert Minotti), June 26, 2026

On the other municipal properties:

"The Municipality identified several municipal properties for consideration during the site selection process. Following Bell's technical and engineering evaluation, Lot 6 065 483 was determined to be the only municipal property that satisfied the technical, engineering, operational and coverage requirements necessary to meet the objectives of the proposed telecommunications system." — Bell (Robert Minotti), June 26, 2026

On the water-tower engineering report:

"The engineering assessment formed the basis for Bell's conclusion that the water tower was unsuitable for the proposed installation. The conclusion of that assessment has been communicated through the consultation process and satisfies the disclosure requirements of CPC-2-0-03. The underlying engineering report is proprietary and is not part of the public consultation record." — Bell (Robert Minotti), June 26, 2026
Editorial note   At the June 10 information session, Bell's representative had said of the same report: "It's right here. […] You can ask to our media relations — I can give you the address if you want." The June 26 letter confirms the report will not be released but does not address the contradiction with that verbal statement.

On the flood zone, Bell explained that the compound's placement relied on a professional survey by a Québec Land Surveyor identifying nearby watercourse, riparian, and marsh boundaries, and confirming the compound was outside them — adding that the provincial flood-zone portal addresses an entire parcel, not the specific compound location. The survey itself was not placed in the public consultation record.

On zoning and the timing of the municipal decision, Bell redirected the questions entirely:

"Questions relating to municipal zoning, municipal resolutions, or the timing of decisions by the Municipality of Ormstown fall within the jurisdiction of the Municipality and are outside Bell's authority and the scope of the ISED CPC-2-0-03 public consultation process." — Bell (Robert Minotti), June 26, 2026

Bell then closed the record:

"Bell now considers the public consultation record complete as of today June 26, 2026. Accordingly, Bell also considers the consultation process with respect to your comments concluded. No further correspondence on the matters addressed above is anticipated." — Bell (Robert Minotti), June 26, 2026
Editorial note   This declaration came fourteen days before the July 10 reply deadline Bell set in Letter 2, and one day after the Observer's June 25 follow-up — a submission filed within the reply period. Under CPC-2-0-03, the consultation is to be considered concluded only after the 21-day reply period has passed without further comment.
